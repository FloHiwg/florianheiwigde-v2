## Intro

We wanted a trip that felt like a real break from winter in Europe and at the same time gave us a mix of city, nature, good food, and something memorable for New Year's Eve.

South Africa ended up being exactly that combination.

Our route was simple but strong:

- Berlin to Cape Town
- Cape Town for the city, wine, and coast
- Hoedspruit and Nyati Safari Lodge for the safari part
- Hoedspruit back to Berlin

In this article, I want to document the trip stop by stop, including where we stayed, what we did each day, what we learned, and what I would do differently next time.

## From Berlin to Cape Town

We flew from Berlin to Cape Town on December 28, 2025 and arrived on December 29, 2025.

The shift from cold European winter to Cape Town summer was probably the fastest possible way to get into holiday mode. Even the arrival already felt like the trip had two very different halves ahead of it: first the city, then the safari.

This was also a good setup for the overall route because Cape Town gave us a relaxed but still active first block before moving into the more structured rhythm of safari days.

## Overall Lessons Learned

- Splitting the trip between Cape Town and safari worked very well.
- Five nights in Cape Town and six nights around the safari felt balanced.
- A trip like this benefits from alternating active days and slower recovery days.

## Final Verdict

South Africa was one of the strongest couple trips we have done because it did not rely on just one type of experience.

It combined city, food, wine, coastline, wildlife, and a genuine sense of contrast in a relatively compact itinerary. That made the whole trip feel richer than a pure city holiday or a pure safari trip on its own.

If I were to recommend this route, I would especially recommend it to couples who want a trip that feels both comfortable and eventful, and who are happy to combine a relaxed city base with a much more immersive nature experience.
